The hosts get the tournament underway on 21 November against Ecuador, with Netherlands and Senegal the other teams drawn in Group A. England have been reunited with the United States, having previously met during the 2010 edition, while Group E boasts heavyweight duo Germany and Spain. There's also a mouthwatering looking Group F, pitting Belgium, Canada, Morocco and Croatia against one another, though there is no obvious 'Group of Death' this time around. FIFA has confirmed the first two rounds of matches will kick off at 1pm, 4pm, 7pm and 10pm local time (10am, 1pm, 4pm and 7pm in the UK and 5am, 8am, 11pm and 2pm US ET). Kick-off times in the final round of group games and knock-out round matches will be at 6pm and 10pm local time (3pm and 7pm UK time and 10am and 2pm US ET). The final is scheduled to kick off at 6pm local time (3pm UK time and 10am ET). Who can still qualify for the World Cup? The 12 UEFA teams will be joined by three more nations - either Wales, Ukraine or Scotland, who are yet to contest their play-off clash. Wales vs Scotland/Ukraine The AFC play-off winner between Australia and UAE will then meet CONMEBOL's Peru in an intercontinental clash. New Zealand, meanwhile, will have to beat CONCACAF's Costa Rica as OFC do not get an automatic qualification spot.
